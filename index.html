What is SECS Sovereign?

SECS Sovereign is a doctrine‑pluggable substrate that guarantees continuity across execution phases. It enforces governed lanes, deterministic collapse paths, and telemetry‑backed propagation — all within a zero‑drift runtime. SECS is not a framework or a library; it is a substrate that governs how systems behave, evolve, and recover.

Why SECS Exists

Distributed systems drift. Execution loses continuity. Governance becomes reactive instead of structural. SECS Sovereign introduces a substrate that preserves state, enforces doctrine, and closes the narrative loop.

  • Continuity as an invariant — execution never loses its story.
  • Governed & ungoverned lanes — explicit separation of controlled and free execution.
  • Telemetry daemon — real‑time propagation traces and continuity markers.
  • Deterministic collapse paths — predictable rollback and recovery logic.

Architecture Overview

The SECS substrate integrates the Governed Lane, Ungoverned Lane, Telemetry Daemon, Orchestrator, and the Cockpit. Together, these components form a continuity‑preserving execution environment that is observable, deterministic, and doctrine‑aligned.
