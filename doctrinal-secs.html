Doctrinal SECS

The doctrinal substrate that shapes behaviour, guarantees determinism, and anchors the Sovereign system.

Continuity

Continuity ensures a verifiable, auditable progression of state across execution windows so systems can be reconciled deterministically.

Deterministic Propagation

Deterministic propagation means identical inputs produce identical state transitions, enabling reproducibility and low‑drift replication.

Governance

Governance defines how changes to governed lanes are proposed, reviewed, and enacted — keeping the substrate aligned with stakeholder intent.

Zero‑Drift

Zero‑drift is the commitment that, barring explicit governance changes, system state remains synchronized across replicas.

Narrative Closure

Narrative closure defines how episodes of activity are concluded and archived as part of the continuity model.

Doctrine as Foundation

The doctrine is the philosophical substrate that guides design choices, operational guarantees, and the project’s public commitments.
